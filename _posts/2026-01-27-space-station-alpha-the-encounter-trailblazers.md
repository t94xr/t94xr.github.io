In Space Station Alpha: The Encounter, players dive into an educational journey through the solar system and the mechanics of space exploration, the planet, mars and fundamentals of flight. The story follows a astronaut who is stationed on the research vessel Space Station Alpha, which has just stumbled upon a mysterious, abandoned alien craft - who is then deployed to explore it.

## Gameplay Overview
- **The Objective:** Explore the station and collect all the hidden alien symbols to access the backend room passed the locked door.
- **Interactive Learning:** Engage with various experiments to trigger educational briefings from mission control.
- **Hands-on Challenges:** Discover new alien symbols by completing puzzles, such as getting the correct order of the planets in the solar system, building a functional biome, exploring Mars, star charts, building a sattelite out of space junk.
- **Exploration:** Tinker with advanced alien technology to understand the fundamental laws of space.

## Development & Release
- **Release Year:** 1996
- **Platforms:** Windows (16-bit) and Macintosh (PowerPC/68k).
- **Publisher:** TeraMedia Ltd. (New Zealand)
- **Developers:** A large team of approximately 55 people, with key direction by [Vivian Horner](https://nickelodeon.fandom.com/wiki/Dr._Vivian_Horner) and [Sally Celmer](https://nickelodeon.fandom.com/wiki/Sally_Celmer).
- **Series:** It was part of the "TimeBlazers" project, which produced other titles like [Devilâ€™s Canyon: A Dinamation Adventure](https://classicreload.com/win3x-devils-canyon.html).

The game famously featured [Eugene Cernan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Cernan), the retired NASA astronaut and the last man to walk on the Moon (Apollo 17). He provided an introductory quote and appeared in the game to inspire players about the future of space exploration and the possibility of alien civilizations

TeraMedia in collaboration with NASA [Young Astronauts Council](https://youngastronautprogram.weebly.com/) produced this game out of the former President of the United States, Ronald Regan era who initiated a push for kids/young adults to gain an interest in Science, Technology, Engineering & Math (which later was referred to as STEM).

## Preservation

I was the first one to archive the Win31/95 files for this game, there was Macintosh Repository page for this game for the PPC/68k version, which was uploaded start of 2017, while my win31/95 installation files were uploaded by me in July 2021. 

I've had this game for years, sitting around, I enjoyed playing it and I knew it was relatively obscure, I didn't realize how obscure it was until I [archived it](1).

*Unfortunately I am unable to get a bit for bit "dd" of the disc with full win,mac & audio data due to scratches on the disc that happened years ago.*
